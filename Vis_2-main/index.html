AUSTRALIAN TOURISM

INTRODUCTION

During the global pandemic, Australia's tourism industry took a significant hit. It has since dedicated large efforts in returning back to a prosperous industry. The following visualization explores various facets of Australian tourism that can be targeted to improve this industry and boost the economy.

TRAVELLER TRENDS ACROSS 5 YEARS

This visualisation depicts the number of foreign travellers to Australia for 2019-2023.

There is a significant decrease in the middle owing to the global pandemic.

Prior to the pandemic, it can be seen a large amount of travellers arrived in December. Thus, more attractions and events should run during the holiday period.

GLOBAL TRAVELLERS

In this visualisation, the nationality of travellers to Australia can be seen.

It can be identifed that the main nationality of tourists to Australia are from: New Zealand, China, America and India.

The Australian tourism industry should use this data to build stronger relationships with these nations to increase the number of visitors and build strong foreign relations.

EXPENDITURE BY STATE

The visualisation on the right depicts the percentage change of expenditure by states in Australia from the 2023 financial year to the 2024 financial year. A more concentrated effort should be given to improving the tourism in states which experienced a decrease in expenditure. A popular solution is an increase in tourism campaigns for that region.

Particularly, more energy should be dedicated to improving tourism in states such as ACT, South Australia, and Western Australia, who all experienced a significant drop in expenditure by tourists.

Additionally, the tourism strategy in Queensland should be analysed as they experienced a steady 1% increase in expenditure.

The tourism practices Northern Territory follows should be analysed and replicated in other states.

VISITOR SPENDING TRENDS

This visualisation depicts the purpose of visit for tourists in Australia in 2023.

The annotations represent revenue for predominant reasons of travel such as: business, education, holiday and visiting friends family.

As holiday is one the major reasons, this suggests the Australian Tourism industry should allocate more funds for family friendly tourist attractions.

Addirionally, as holiday is another major reason for travel to Australia, more resources should be allocated in offering authentic Australian experiences.
